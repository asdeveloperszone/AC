About C Language

C is a powerful general-purpose programming language developed in 1972 by Dennis Ritchie at Bell Labs. It's the backbone of operating systems like Linux and Unix, and plays a crucial role in Android development through the NDK (Native Development Kit). C's efficiency, portability, and direct hardware access make it indispensable in system programming, embedded systems, and performance-critical applications.
